Chess is a board game for two players. It is played in a square board, made of 64 smaller squares, with eight squares on each side. Each player starts with sixteen pieces: eight pawns, two knights, two bishops, two rooks, one queen and one king. The goal of the game is for each player to try and checkmate the king of the opponent. Checkmate is a threat ('check') to the opposing king which no move can stop. It ends the game.

The openings of a chess game is important; it determines if you are going to play defensive or attacking. Many of the openings have been named and used for hundreds of years. To be successful in chess it helps to understand the openings and the theory behind them. If you’re just starting you may be wondering what to study first and what to learn, well it’s good to study openings first because in every game you're going to need them. You should focus on learning the concepts behind the opernings. Some of the most consequently used openings for white are: Kings gambit, Queen's gambit, Ruy Lopez and the English. For black a common but good openings are: Sicilian defence, French defense, Scandinavian and Slav. It is important for the player to do a strategy that works for them, if you’re a defensive player doing a defensive opening and if you’re an attacking player to do an attacking opening.
